# Chapter 11 — Thermodynamics

<ConceptMeta chapter="11" section="11.1–11.9" page="252–275" />

Thermodynamics governs how energy is converted between heat and work — the foundation of all engines, refrigerators and power plants.

## Concepts in This Chapter

- [Zeroth & First Law](./first-law) — Internal energy, heat, work and their relationship
- [Thermodynamic Processes](./thermodynamic-processes) — Isothermal, adiabatic, isochoric, isobaric
- [Second Law & Entropy](./second-law) — Why heat flows one way and engines are never 100% efficient
- [Heat Engines & Refrigerators](./heat-engines) — Work from heat; Carnot cycle
- [Carnot Theorem](./carnot-theorem) — The maximum possible efficiency

## Why This Matters

Every power station, car engine, refrigerator and air conditioner is a thermodynamic machine. The laws of thermodynamics set absolute limits on what is possible.
