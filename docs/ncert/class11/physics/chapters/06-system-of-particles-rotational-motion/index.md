# Chapter 6 — System of Particles & Rotational Motion

<ConceptMeta chapter="6" section="6.1–6.8" page="102–136" />

This chapter moves from a single particle to **systems of particles and rigid bodies**. The key insight: every complex motion of a rigid body can be split into (1) translation of the centre of mass and (2) rotation about the centre of mass. Master those two, and you can analyse everything from a spinning top to a rolling wheel.

Work through the concepts in order. Each page follows the same plan: the idea in plain words, the formula with symbols named, a real-world example, a home experiment, a "what if" to think about, and a quick check.

## Concepts in this chapter

1. [Centre of Mass](./centre-of-mass) — the one point that behaves like a particle.
2. [Torque](./torque) — rotational equivalent of force.
3. [Angular Momentum](./angular-momentum) — rotational equivalent of linear momentum.
4. [Moment of Inertia](./moment-of-inertia) — rotational equivalent of mass.
5. [Rolling Motion](./rolling-motion) — translation and rotation combined.

## The one-line summary

> A rigid body's motion is the sum of its centre-of-mass translation and its rotation about that centre — and angular momentum, like linear momentum, is conserved when no external torque acts.
