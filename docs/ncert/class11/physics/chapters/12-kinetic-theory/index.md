# Chapter 12 — Kinetic Theory

<ConceptMeta chapter="12" section="12.1–12.6" page="280–298" />

Kinetic theory explains macroscopic properties of gases (pressure, temperature, specific heat) from the microscopic motion of molecules.

## Concepts in This Chapter

- [Kinetic Theory of Gases](./kinetic-theory-gases) — Pressure from molecular collisions; the ideal gas
- [Speed of Gas Molecules](./speed-of-molecules) — rms, average and most probable speeds
- [Law of Equipartition of Energy](./equipartition) — Energy distributed equally among degrees of freedom
- [Mean Free Path](./mean-free-path) — Average distance between collisions

## Why This Matters

Kinetic theory bridges the gap between microscopic physics (atoms) and macroscopic thermodynamics — showing that temperature is literally the motion of molecules.
