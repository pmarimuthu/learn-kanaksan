# Chapter 9 — Mechanical Properties of Fluids

<ConceptMeta chapter="9" section="9.1–9.8" page="190–218" />

Fluids (liquids and gases) cannot sustain shear stress — they flow. This chapter covers pressure, buoyancy, viscosity, surface tension and Bernoulli's principle.

## Concepts in This Chapter

- [Pressure & Pascal's Law](./pressure-pascals-law) — Pressure in a fluid and how it transmits force
- [Archimedes' Principle](./archimedes-principle) — Why things float or sink
- [Viscosity & Stokes' Law](./viscosity) — Internal friction in flowing fluids
- [Surface Tension](./surface-tension) — The skin on a liquid surface
- [Bernoulli's Theorem](./bernoullis-theorem) — Energy conservation in flowing fluids

## Why This Matters

From hydraulic brakes to aircraft wings, from soap bubbles to blood flow, fluid mechanics underpins much of modern technology.
