# Chapter 4 — Laws of Motion

<ConceptMeta chapter="4" section="4.1–4.10" page="55–83" />

This chapter answers one big question: **what makes things speed up, slow down, or change direction?** The surprising answer from Galileo and Newton is that motion does *not* need a cause — only a *change* in motion does. That change is called **force**.

Work through the concepts in order. Each page follows the same plan: the idea in plain words, the formula with symbols named, a real-world example, a home experiment, a "what if" to think about, and a quick check.

## Concepts in this chapter

1. [Inertia & the First Law](./inertia-and-first-law) — why things keep doing what they're doing.
2. [Momentum & the Second Law](./momentum-and-second-law) — how force actually changes motion.
3. [Impulse](./impulse) — why a longer push is gentler.
4. [The Third Law](./third-law) — every push has an equal push back.
5. [Conservation of Momentum](./conservation-of-momentum) — why a gun recoils and a rocket flies.
6. [Equilibrium of a Particle](./equilibrium-of-a-particle) — when all forces cancel.
7. [Friction](./friction) — the force that both helps and hinders.
8. [Circular Motion](./circular-motion) — why roads are banked and water stays in a swung bucket.

## The one-line summary

> An object's motion changes only when a net external force acts on it, and that change is exactly **F = ma**. Forces always come in equal and opposite pairs.
