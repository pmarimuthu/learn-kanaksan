# Chapter 1 — Units and Measurement

<ConceptMeta chapter="1" section="1.1–1.6" page="1–13" />

Physics begins with **measurement** — and measurement only means something if everyone agrees on the units. This chapter sets up the language every later chapter uses: the SI units, how to report a measurement honestly with significant figures, and how the *dimensions* of a quantity let you check (and even guess) formulas.

Work through the concepts in order. Each page follows the same plan: predict, understand, see it, formalize, connect to real life, try it, test yourself, revise, and explore trusted sources.

## Concepts in this chapter

1. [Physical Quantities & SI Units](./physical-quantities-and-si-units) — the seven base units everything is built from.
2. [Significant Figures](./significant-figures) — reporting a measurement honestly.
3. [Dimensions of Physical Quantities](./dimensions-of-physical-quantities) — the M, L, T fingerprint of every quantity.
4. [Dimensional Analysis](./dimensional-analysis) — checking and building formulas with dimensions.

## The one-line summary

> Measure against agreed **SI units**, report only the digits you really know (**significant figures**), and remember every quantity has **dimensions** that must match on both sides of any correct equation.
