# Chapter 8 — Mechanical Properties of Solids

<ConceptMeta chapter="8" section="8.1–8.6" page="168–187" />

Solids resist change in shape and size. This chapter explores how materials deform under applied forces and the fundamental laws that govern elastic behaviour.

## Concepts in This Chapter

- [Stress and Strain](./stress-and-strain) — What stretching, squeezing and shearing do to a solid
- [Hooke's Law](./hookes-law) — The proportional region of elastic behaviour
- [Elastic Moduli](./elastic-moduli) — Young's, shear and bulk modulus
- [Stress–Strain Curve](./stress-strain-curve) — Elastic limit, yield point and breaking stress
- [Applications of Elasticity](./applications) — Bridges, bones, springs, rubber

## Why This Matters

Every structure you build or sit in — a bridge, a chair, a building — works because engineers have matched the material to the forces. Stress and strain are the language of structural safety.
