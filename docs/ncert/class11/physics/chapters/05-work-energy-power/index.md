# Chapter 5 — Work, Energy and Power

<ConceptMeta chapter="5" section="5.1–5.9" page="74–101" />

This chapter answers a deceptively simple question: **what does a force actually accomplish?** The answer is *work* — and the capacity to do work is *energy*. Once you see that energy is conserved (never created or destroyed, only transformed), you have one of the most powerful tools in all of physics.

Work through the concepts in order. Each page follows the same plan: the idea in plain words, the formula with symbols named, a real-world example, a home experiment, a "what if" to think about, and a quick check.

## Concepts in this chapter

1. [Work](./work) — when a force actually does something useful.
2. [Kinetic Energy & the Work–Energy Theorem](./kinetic-energy) — motion as stored work.
3. [Potential Energy](./potential-energy) — stored energy waiting to be released.
4. [Conservation of Mechanical Energy](./conservation-of-energy) — why a pendulum keeps swinging.
5. [Power](./power) — how quickly work gets done.
6. [Collisions](./collisions) — elastic, inelastic, and why momentum and energy tell different stories.

## The one-line summary

> When a force moves an object, it does *work* which changes the object's *energy*; and the total mechanical energy of an isolated system never changes — it only converts between kinetic and potential forms.
