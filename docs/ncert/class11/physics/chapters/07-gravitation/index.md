# Chapter 7 — Gravitation

<ConceptMeta chapter="7" section="7.1–7.8" page="137–165" />

Gravity is the weakest of the four fundamental forces — yet it rules the universe at large scales. This chapter starts with Newton's universal law and builds up to satellite orbits, escape velocity, and Kepler's celebrated laws of planetary motion.

Work through the concepts in order. Each page follows the same plan: the idea in plain words, the formula with symbols named, a real-world example, a home experiment, a "what if" to think about, and a quick check.

## Concepts in this chapter

1. [Universal Law of Gravitation](./universal-law) — every mass attracts every other mass.
2. [Gravitational Field & Potential](./gravitational-field) — describing gravity in space.
3. [Escape Velocity](./escape-velocity) — the speed needed to leave a planet's gravity.
4. [Satellites & Orbital Motion](./satellites) — how and why satellites orbit.
5. [Mission: Launch a Satellite](./mission-launch-a-satellite) — an interactive launch simulator. Discover orbit, crash and escape yourself before the formulas.
6. [Kepler's Laws](./keplers-laws) — the rules planets obey.

## The one-line summary

> Every pair of masses attracts each other with a force that falls off as the square of the distance — and all of planetary mechanics follows from that single law.
