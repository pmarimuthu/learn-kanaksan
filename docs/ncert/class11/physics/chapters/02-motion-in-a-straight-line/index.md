# Chapter 2 — Motion in a Straight Line

<ConceptMeta chapter="2" section="2.1-2.5" page="14-27" />

This chapter is about **the language of motion**. Before we can explain *why* things move (that's Newton, Chapter 4), we need precise words to *describe* how they move. The concepts here — position, displacement, velocity, acceleration — are the vocabulary every physicist and engineer uses every day.

Work through the concepts in order. Each page follows the same plan: the idea in plain words, the formula with symbols named, a real-world example, a home experiment, a "what if" to think about, and a quick check.

## Concepts in this chapter

1. [Position, Displacement & Path Length](./displacement) — the difference between how far you travelled and how far you ended up.
2. [Velocity & Speed](./velocity-and-speed) — rate of change of position, and why direction matters.
3. [Acceleration](./acceleration) — how quickly velocity changes.
4. [Equations of Motion](./equations-of-motion) — three formulas that describe any uniform acceleration.
5. [Relative Velocity](./relative-velocity) — motion is always measured from some reference point.

## The one-line summary

> Motion in a straight line is fully described by position, velocity, and acceleration — and the three kinematic equations connect them when acceleration is constant.
