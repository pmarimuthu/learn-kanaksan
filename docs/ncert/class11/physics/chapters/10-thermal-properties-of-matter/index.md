# Chapter 10 — Thermal Properties of Matter

<ConceptMeta chapter="10" section="10.1–10.8" page="224–245" />

Heat and temperature govern how matter expands, stores energy, and transfers heat to its surroundings.

## Concepts in This Chapter

- [Temperature & Heat](./temperature-and-heat) — Scales, zeroth law, and the difference between heat and temperature
- [Thermal Expansion](./thermal-expansion) — Solids, liquids and gases expand when heated
- [Calorimetry](./calorimetry) — Measuring heat; specific and latent heat
- [Heat Transfer](./heat-transfer) — Conduction, convection and radiation
- [Newton's Law of Cooling](./newtons-law-of-cooling) — How objects cool towards room temperature

## Why This Matters

Thermal properties determine everything from why bridges have expansion joints to how a thermos flask keeps your tea hot.
